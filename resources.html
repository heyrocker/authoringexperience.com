Resources

January 30, 2025 by Greg Dunlap

If you've made it here, then you purchased a copy of Designing Content Authoring Experiences and are looking for an online version of the resources. First off, thank you for your support! I greatly appreciate it.

There are three things you can find here.

  • A downloadable zip file containing all the screenshots from the book, which you can download and blow up at your leisure
  • All the links in each chapter, easily clickable for your own research.
  • All the links in the Resources section, easily clickable for your own research.

I hope you find this all helpful!

Resources

The Authoring Experience

  • Web Content Management: Systems, Features, and Best Practices by Deane Barker

  • The Web Project Guide by Corey Vilhauer and Deane Barker

Structure vs. Flexibility

  • Real World Content Modeling by Deane Barker

  • Designing Connected Content: Plan and Model Digital Products for Today and Tomorrow by Mike Atherton and Carrie Hane

  • “Page Builders vs. Traditional CMS” – an episode of the Cutting Edge Web Development podcast, featuring Jeff Eaton and Karen McGrane

Understanding Your Organization

  • There are various models to understand phases of content maturity, but I like this one from Content Strategy Inc.

Understanding Your Authors

  • Just Enough Research by Erika Hall

  • 18F Methods is a collection of tools and exercises that can be used to gather information from users and identify needs for a digital project.

Prototyping

  • Nu Board portable white boards

  • Optimal Workshop is a popular tool for doing click-testing as well as other types of user testing.

Designing Forms

  • Forms That Work: Designing Web Forms for Usability by Steve Kruger, Caroline Jarrett, and Gerry Gaffney

  • Form Design Patterns by Adam Silver. Adam also has an excellent newsletter that often talks about form design, which you can sign up for at his website.

  • Designing UX: Forms by Jessica Enders

  • Federal Plain Language Guidelines

  • Center for Plain Language

  • “6 Ways to Reduce Cognitive Load for a Better UI” by Vitor Guerra

  • “Exploring the Gestalt Principles of Design” by Cameron Chapman

  • “How to Provide Accessible Form Error Identification” by Level Access

Content Administration

  • The interface for choosing a content type was developed by Lullabot for the state of Georgia. It was released as a freely downloadable module for Drupal called Type Tray, and you can read more about it on the state’s website.

Accessibility

  • “4 Major Patterns for Accessible Drag and Drop” by Jesse Hausler

  • We 4 Authors Cluster was a pilot podcast on web accessibility for web authoring tools producers and communities led by Funka, a Swedish agency specializing in accessibility services. This playlist shows examples of how different authoring tasks could be made more accessible.

  • “Introduction to Web Accessibility”. W3C offers a free online digital accessibility foundations course.

  • “Accessibility for Content Designers”. The General Services Administration created a quick-start guide for embedding accessibility and inclusive design practices into your team’s workflow that offers a lot of useful high-level tips for designers and developers.

Support

  • Leading Content Design by Rachel McConnell

  • From Solo to Scaled: Building a Sustainable Content Strategy Practice by Natalie Marie Dunbar
